Wrapped Reichstag was a monumental art installation by the renowned artist duo Christo and Jeanne-Claude, completed in 1995. The project involved covering Berlin’s historic Reichstag building in 100,000 square meters of silver fabric, secured by 15 kilometers of blue rope.

The installation transformed the Reichstag into a shimmering, ephemeral structure, altering its presence and inviting the public to engage with it in a new way. The project was not merely aesthetic; it symbolized political change, reflecting Germany’s reunification and the evolving role of its democratic institutions.

As with many of Christo and Jeanne-Claude’s works, Wrapped Reichstag was temporary, lasting just two weeks. However, its impact was profound, drawing millions of visitors and sparking discussions about history, transformation, and the relationship between architecture and art.

Today, Wrapped Reichstag is remembered as one of the most significant site-specific artworks of the 20th century, demonstrating how art can engage with public space, history, and collective memory in powerful and unexpected ways.
