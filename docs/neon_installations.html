Neon installations have played a transformative role in urban environments, blending elements of art, architecture, and commercial signage to create immersive visual experiences. Emerging in the early 20th century, neon lighting quickly became a defining feature of cityscapes, particularly in metropolises like Tokyo, Las Vegas, and Hong Kong.

Artists and designers have embraced neon as a medium for conceptual and experiential works. Figures such as Dan Flavin and Tracey Emin have used neon in fine art, while urban interventions and large-scale installations have explored themes of consumer culture, nostalgia, and the interplay between artificial and natural light.

Neon installations often redefine how people interact with public spaces, creating surreal and atmospheric environments. They can transform an ordinary street into a dynamic, dreamlike setting, influencing movement and perception. In addition to art, neon has also been utilized in architectural projects, enhancing facades, interiors, and public landmarks with vibrant, luminous compositions.

While traditional neon lighting has declined due to the rise of LED technology, its cultural and aesthetic impact remains significant.
