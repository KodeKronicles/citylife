Since its inception in the 1970s, Arcosanti has served as both a functioning settlement and a learning center, where students and volunteers contribute to its ongoing development. The city features a series of domes, amphitheaters, and mixed-use structures designed to optimize energy efficiency and resource utilization. Passive solar heating, water collection systems, and organic farming initiatives are key components of its sustainability strategy.

Arcosanti is also a cultural and educational hub, hosting workshops, performances, and discussions on sustainable architecture and urban planning. Its design encourages a communal way of living, with shared workspaces and collaborative projects that emphasize the importance of social cohesion in sustainable development.

Despite its small scale, Arcosanti remains a powerful symbol in the discourse on urban sustainability. It demonstrates that cities can be designed to be more compact, efficient, and environmentally friendly without sacrificing quality of life. The project continues to inspire architects, planners, and environmentalists seeking alternatives to conventional urban expansion.

As climate change and rapid urbanization challenge traditional city models, the principles behind Arcosanti become increasingly relevant. By studying its successes and limitations, future urban developments can incorporate arcological elements, paving the way for more harmonious and resilient cities worldwide.
