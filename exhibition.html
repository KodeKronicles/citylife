The Exhibition

Exploring the Boundaries of Architecture

Architecture is more than just buildings—it is a reflection of human ambition, societal shifts, and technological evolution. From utopian visions to dystopian realities, from radical experiments to practical innovations, architectural ideas shape and redefine how we interact with the world. This exhibition delves into five core narratives, each offering a unique lens on how cities, spaces, and ideas evolve:

  • In Space – How visionary projects are shaped by geography and spatial constraints.
  • In Time – How architecture responds to and influences historical changes.
  • In Action – The transition from speculative concepts to real-world applications.
  • In Context – The role of architecture within urbanism, housing, art, and technology.
  • In Ideals – The principles and philosophies that guide architectural thinking.

Explore the past, present, and future of architecture—where boundaries are challenged, cities are imagined, and reality is constantly reshaped.

In Space

How Architecture Redefines Geography

Cities are not static—they expand, contract, and evolve based on geography, technology, and political forces. From floating metropolises to subterranean habitats, from hyper-dense informal settlements to futuristic AI-generated designs, urban concepts are shaped by space itself. This section explores visionary projects and radical interventions across different regions of the world. Some propose entirely new ways of living, while others challenge existing spatial hierarchies and geopolitical boundaries.
